01 Identity: Guardians in the Rubble

In ancient lore, the Yaksha were guardians of the earth—silent sentinels watching over what humans could not see. YAKSHA inherits that name for a modern frontier: collapsed structures, toxic atmospheres, and heat that melts steel.

We don’t chase headlines. We build machines that move into places where sending another human would mean adding one more life to the list at risk.

02 The Problem: Human Limitation

Fire, radiation, structural instability—these aren’t just “difficult conditions.” They are environments designed by physics to reject human presence. Even the best rescue teams are constrained by heat tolerance, breathing time, visibility, and debris.

Every minute a responder spends finding a safe path is a minute not spent finding a survivor. Traditional methods accept this as unavoidable lag. We don’t.

03 Synergy: Human + Machine

YAKSHA is not here to replace rescuers. We are here to give them unbreakable hands, tireless legs, and eyes that do not blink in smoke.

Our rovers are built as extensions of the incident commander’s intent: they push ahead into the unknown, mapping risk, streaming multi-spectral data, and turning chaos into a navigable plan. The human stays in command; the machine absorbs the impact.

04 Our DNA: Built at the Intersection

YAKSHA is a convergence of aerospace engineering, AI research, and field operations. Our teams have designed systems that orbit the planet, models that predict risk, and protocols that have been tested in real siren-filled nights.

The Engineers

Structures, thermals, and failure modes engineered for 500°C+ exposure and high‑G impact resilience.

The Strategists

Former disaster response leads who design systems around incident command realities—not lab assumptions.

The Intelligence

Situational AI that fuses lidar, gas, thermal, and video feeds into a single evolving picture of risk.
